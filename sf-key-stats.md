# SF Homelessness — Key Shareable Stats

*One-page summary pulled from [sf-street-population.md](sf-street-population.md) (full sourcing there). Compiled June 23, 2026.*

> **Read the denominators.** Population totals are the **2026** count (latest). Demographics, mental-health, and drug percentages are from the **2024** count/survey (latest with that detail) and describe **all people experiencing homelessness — sheltered + unsheltered combined** (we don't have clean unsheltered-only cuts). "Sheltered" = *temporary* shelter; **sheltered ≠ housed**. Most percentages are **self-reported**.

---

## 1. How many — and where they sleep (2026, latest)

| | People | Share |
|---|---|---|
| **Total homeless, one night** | **7,973** | 100% |
| **— Unsheltered** (street, vehicle, tent) | **3,400** | **43%** |
| **— Sheltered** (temporary shelter / transitional housing) | **4,573** | 57% |

- **Over a full year**, the City serves **~27,000 people** (≈3× the one-night number). Shelter and supportive housing are both **~90% full**, and **~8% of people who exit return to homelessness** — placements mostly stick.
- **Chronically homeless: ~36%** (2,989 in 2024) — *and a majority (56%) of them are sheltered, not on the street.*
- *2024 for comparison: 8,323 total / 4,354 unsheltered. The 2026 count changed methodology — counted in the morning (prior counts were overnight) and asked people their housing status rather than judging it visually — so the year-over-year drop isn't clean.*

---

## 2. Demographics (2024, all homeless)

- **Age:** ~47% are **25–44**; 14% are 18–24; 7% under 18; 15% are 55+.
- **Gender:** **59% men**, 32% women, ~6% transgender / non-binary.
- **Race/ethnicity:** **24% Black** (~5× their share of the city), **34% Hispanic/Latino**, 29% White, 4% Asian.
- **LGBTQ+:** **28%** — about 2.3× the citywide share (~12%).

---

## 3. Mental health

- **51% self-report a psychiatric or emotional condition** (2024 PIT survey, all homeless).
- Of those, a hard core have a **diagnosed serious mental illness** (psychosis, bipolar, severe depression w/ hospitalization): **~8,300 people** identified in city clinical data — the disabling, "can't function / off meds" group.
- *Caveat: the 51% is partly reactive distress from being homeless; the ~8,300 diagnosed figure is the firmer "serious" number.*

---

## 4. Drug & alcohol use

- **51% self-report drug or alcohol use** as a health condition (2024 PIT survey, all homeless).
- **Wider context:** SF has an estimated **~37,500 hard drug users** citywide — but **~90% are housed**; only **~10% are unsheltered**. The street drug scene is the visible tip of a largely *housed* population.

---

## 5. Drug mix — three different pictures

There is no single "what drug" number; it depends on what you measure:

| Measure | Picture |
|---|---|
| **Who enters treatment** | **Opioids, methamphetamine, and alcohol co-lead** — not opioid-only |
| **Who dies (overdose)** | **Fentanyl drives ~71–75% of deaths**; meth & cocaine show up as co-factors in ~half |
| **The quiet one** | **Meth** is major but under-served — no medication exists to treat it (unlike opioids/alcohol) |

*Overdose deaths are falling (810 in 2023 → ~626 in 2025), but largely because of Narcan saturation — not because fewer people are using.*

---
